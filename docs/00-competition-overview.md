## Theme (2026)
> Intelligent virtual assistant supporting in-depth analysis and retrieval of information from large-scale **Multimedia** data (images, audio, text, video).

This builds on the 2024 and 2025 themes which centered on **event retrieval from large video collections**. The 2026 edition broadens the data modality to **full multimedia** while keeping the same interactive-retrieval evaluation style.

## Format - Inherited from VBS & LSC
The competition format is taken from two international competitions:
- **Video Browser Showdown (VBS)** - <https://videobrowsershowdown.org/>
- **Lifelog Search Challenge (LSC)** - <http://lsc.dcu.ie/>

Senior VBS / LSC organisers sit on the AI Challenge jury, so format expectations are very close to those events.

## Task: Known-Item Search (KIS)
Given a description, find the **exact** target segment inside a large multimedia corpus.

### Two query modalities
1. **Textual KIS** - a natural-language description in Vietnamese.
   - Preliminary round: the description is shown in full upfront.
   - Final round: the description is revealed **incrementally**. Teams may submit early for more points (if correct) or wait for more clues (lower risk, fewer points).
2. **Video KIS** - a short clip (≤ 20 seconds) extracted from the source corpus.
   - Final round only.
   - **Cameras / screenshots of the clip are FORBIDDEN** - teams must describe what they saw and search with their tool.

## What Wins
- The tool must let a **human operator** retrieve the correct item quickly.
- Scoring rewards **speed** and **first-try accuracy**. Wrong submissions are penalised.
- The judges may audit your tool to ensure your hits came from it (no manual lookup tricks).
