Theme & Problem

The 2026 theme is the most ambitious of the AI Challenge series so far. Past editions focused on event retrieval from video; this year the scope widens to multimedia (images, audio, text, video) and the deliverable is framed as an intelligent virtual assistant.

Theme statement

Official theme (Vietnamese)

Trợ lý ảo thông minh hỗ trợ phân tích và truy xuất thông tin chuyên sâu trong dữ liệu lớn Multimedia (hình ảnh, âm thanh, văn bản, video).

"An intelligent virtual assistant supporting in-depth analysis and retrieval of information from large-scale multimedia data (images, audio, text, video)."

Three keywords reshape the problem versus 2022-2024:

trợ lý ảo thông minh
Intelligent virtual assistant. Implies a natural-language, possibly conversational, LLM-driven interface - not just a search box.
phân tích chuyên sâu
In-depth analysis. Beyond find-and-stop: the system should help reason about content (Q&A, summarisation, comparison).
multimedia
Full multimedia scope. Images, audio, text, video - not video-only as in 2024.

Core problem - Known-Item Search (KIS)

Regardless of the new theme wording, the AI Challenge inherits its evaluation format from Video Browser Showdown (VBS) and Lifelog Search Challenge (LSC). The atomic task is Known-Item Search:

  • Textual KIS: the jury provides a Vietnamese description; the team must locate the exact target. Preliminary round shows the full description upfront. Final round reveals it incrementally - rewarding teams who submit early and correctly.
  • Video KIS: the jury plays a clip (≤ 20 s) from the corpus. Teams are forbidden from recording or screenshotting the clip - they must describe what they saw via the tool's interface.
The contest does not award a fully automated solver. We must ship an interactive tool that lets a human operator find answers fast. The operator races the clock; wrong submissions compound penalties. First-try accuracy matters more than raw recall.

Engineering priorities follow:

  1. Latency - end-to-end search under 200 ms (P95) on the fast path.
  2. Top-12 recall - the gold frame must be in the operator's visible region.
  3. Keyboard-first UI - shortcuts, command palette, virtualised grid.
  4. Vietnamese support - OCR, ASR, query parsing with correct diacritics.
  5. Audit-ready logs - the jury may demand a live replay of any submission.

Tech Stack - What & Why

The menu of technologies every team member must understand. Each card explains what it is, why we chose it given the theme, what alternatives we considered, and where it fits.

VL

3.1 Vision-Language Foundations

The embedding models that map text and frames into a shared space - the heart of retrieval.

OpenCLIP ViT-L/14 (LAION-2B)

EMBEDDING · PRIMARY

A 304 M-parameter image-text encoder trained on 2 B web pairs - our default retrieval model.

What is it?

An open re-implementation of OpenAI's CLIP trained on LAION-2B. Produces 768-dim L2-normalised embeddings where dot product approximates semantic similarity between a sentence and an image.

Why we chose it
  • Battle-tested on Vietnamese news in AI Challenge 2024 papers.
  • Stronger than ViT-B/16 (BTC baseline) by ~5-8 points on Recall@10.
  • Open weights: no API quota, runs offline on a 24 GB GPU.
  • Permissive licence: usable for the public-sharing requirement post-event.
Alternatives
  • CLIP ViT-B/16 (BTC pre-computed) - keep as baseline, ViT-L wins on retrieval.
  • EVA-CLIP-G - stronger but 1.4 B params, latency at risk.
Use in our system

Encode every keyframe at ingest. Encode query text at runtime. Search FAISS HNSW on dot product.

SigLIP SO400M

EMBEDDING · CHALLENGER

Google's sigmoid-loss multilingual encoder; often beats CLIP on retrieval.

What is it?

Replaces CLIP's softmax contrastive loss with sigmoid loss applied independently to every image-text pair. Removes the need for very large batches and scales to longer text. SO400M is the 400 M-parameter shape-optimised checkpoint.

Why we chose it
  • Better text understanding than CLIP for longer Vietnamese sentences.
  • Stronger zero-shot retrieval at the same parameter budget.
  • Multilingual training handles Vietnamese tokens better out of the box.
Alternatives
  • SigLIP base - smaller, faster, but loses ~3-4 points Recall@10.
  • SigLIP-2 - newer, evaluate when stable.
Use in our system

Production challenger to OpenCLIP. Index the same keyframes with both; operator switches via the M shortcut.

BLIP-2

CAPTIONING

A vision-language model that writes a rich English caption for every keyframe.

What is it?

Bridges a frozen vision encoder and a frozen language model via a small Q-Former adapter. Can caption images, answer visual questions, and ground entities.

Why we chose it
  • Richer text per keyframe than raw object classes; lets BM25 catch keyframes CLIP misses.
  • Stable English output we can feed to the LLM rerank layer (T2).
  • Lightweight inference in FP16 on a single GPU.
Alternatives
  • LLaVA-OneVision - more capable, especially on video; consider for Phase 2.
  • InstructBLIP - instruction-tuned; marginal gains for our task.
Use in our system

Caption every keyframe at ingest. Captions become a sparse text field indexed with BM25. Also feed captions into LLM rerank candidates.

EVA-CLIP / LLaVA-OneVision

RESERVE

Higher-capacity vision-language models held in reserve for the final round.

What are they?

EVA-CLIP scales the CLIP recipe to billions of parameters with refined initialisation, yielding state-of-the-art zero-shot retrieval. LLaVA-OneVision unifies image, multi-image, and short-video understanding behind a multimodal LLM.

Why we chose them
  • Quality ceiling: when small models plateau, we can climb further.
  • Video understanding in LLaVA-OneVision helps the Video-KIS describe-flow.
Cost & risk
  • Add 2-5 s latency per query - reserve for the final round only.
  • Require > 40 GB GPU memory or sharding.
Use in our system

Tier-T3 only. Activated on demand for the hardest queries. Not on the fast path.

EX

3.2 Visual & Audio Extraction

Models that turn raw multimedia into structured, searchable signals.

YOLOv10

OBJECT DETECTION

Real-time object detection that re-scans every keyframe with up-to-date class accuracy.

What is it?

The 2024 iteration of the YOLO family, removing non-maximum suppression and improving small-object accuracy on COCO. Inference is sub-millisecond on a modern GPU.

Why we chose it
  • Modern class accuracy beats the BTC-provided Faster R-CNN baseline on common classes.
  • Speed: re-detect 300 h of keyframes in a few hours.
  • Augments BTC concepts with our own labels - we are not locked in.
Alternatives
  • Faster R-CNN (BTC-provided) - already shipped, but 600 fixed labels.
  • YOLOv8 - stable but slightly behind in accuracy.
Use in our system

Backbone of the closed-vocabulary object filter. Detections feed an inverted index keyed by class.

Grounding DINO

OPEN-VOCAB DETECTION

Detect arbitrary visual concepts described in natural language.

What is it?

An open-set object detector that accepts free-form text prompts ("Vietnamese national flag", "elderly farmer with conical hat") and returns bounding boxes. Bridges the gap between fixed-class detection and CLIP-style retrieval.

Why we chose it
  • Operator-typed filters: the contestant types a phrase, the system grounds it.
  • Coverage: catches culturally specific objects (nón lá, áo dài, đèn lồng) absent from COCO.
  • Complements CLIP: gives spatial localisation in addition to retrieval.
Alternatives
  • OWL-ViT - similar concept, weaker on small objects.
  • Detic - large vocabulary but fixed class set.
Use in our system

Operator-triggered. The UI exposes a "filter by visual concept" text box backed by Grounding DINO.

PaddleOCR (PP-OCRv4)

OCR · PRIMARY

The best general-purpose OCR for Vietnamese diacritics.

What is it?

Baidu's PaddleOCR stack: text detection (DBNet-style) followed by CRNN recognition, with a small angle classifier. PP-OCRv4 added training data and quality improvements over v3.

Why we chose it
  • Vietnamese support is genuinely good; diacritics survive better than in EasyOCR.
  • Fast on CPU - no scarce GPU time for OCR.
  • News graphics ready: handles burnt-in chyrons and tickers.
Alternatives
  • EasyOCR - simpler API but worse on Vietnamese tonal marks.
  • Tesseract - mature, but Vietnamese quality lags far behind.
Use in our system

Run on every keyframe at ingest. Output stored in data/metadata/ocr.parquet, indexed by BM25.

PhoWhisper + faster-whisper

ASR · PRIMARY

VinAI's Whisper fine-tuned on Vietnamese broadcast audio, accelerated with CTranslate2.

What is it?

PhoWhisper is OpenAI's Whisper architecture fine-tuned by VinAI on Vietnamese news, interviews, and conversation. faster-whisper wraps it in CTranslate2 for 4-5x speedup with the same quality. pyannote.audio provides voice-activity detection so we transcribe only the speech regions.

Why we chose it
  • Domain match: news Vietnamese is its strongest setting.
  • Better diacritics than vanilla Whisper on regional accents.
  • Speed: cuts 300 h of audio transcription from days to hours.
  • VAD avoids hallucinated transcripts on non-speech.
Alternatives
  • Whisper large-v3 - general-purpose; runs as fallback.
  • wav2vec2-base-vietnamese - smaller, weaker, useful for streaming.
Use in our system

Pipeline: VAD → segments → faster-whisper → diacritic post-processing. Output in data/metadata/asr.parquet with millisecond-precise timestamps.

TransNetV2

SHOT BOUNDARY

Lightweight neural shot-boundary detector to extract keyframes.

What is it?

A small 3D-CNN that predicts shot transitions frame-by-frame. We use it (or BTC-provided keyframes when sufficient) to pick a representative frame per shot.

Why we chose it
  • Quality: catches soft transitions BTC's default may miss.
  • Speed: faster than running CLIP on every frame.
  • Open licence: usable in submitted artefacts.
Alternative
  • PySceneDetect - classical histogram-based; faster but less accurate on dissolves.
Use in our system

If BTC keyframes are insufficient, densify with TransNetV2. One keyframe per shot, plus BTC defaults.

RE

3.3 Retrieval Engine

The fast-path engine: dense vectors + sparse text + fusion.

FAISS (HNSW)

ANN · PRIMARY

Facebook AI Similarity Search - the de facto open ANN library.

What is it?

A C++ library with Python bindings for billion-scale similarity search. HNSW (Hierarchical Navigable Small World) is one of its index types - a multi-layer graph supporting sub-millisecond retrieval with high recall.

Why we chose it
  • P95 < 5 ms on our corpus size (1-3 M keyframes).
  • Tuneable recall vs latency via efSearch at query time.
  • Memory-mapped: index stays on disk, loads on demand.
  • Battle-tested in every VBS / LSC winner of the last five years.
Alternatives
  • FAISS IVF-PQ - smaller memory but slower; reserve for emergencies.
  • ScaNN - Google's alternative, slightly faster but harder to deploy.
Use in our system

Default: HNSW with M=32, efConstruction=200, efSearch=64. Loaded once at backend startup.

Qdrant

ANN · BACKUP

Open-source vector database with filtering and payload support.

What is it?

A Rust-based vector database with HNSW under the hood plus structured payload filtering (e.g. "vectors where video_id IN [...]"). Talks over HTTP / gRPC.

Why we chose it
  • Payload filters: vector search + structured constraints (year, video, object class) in one round trip.
  • Hot reload: easier to swap models than re-baking a FAISS file.
  • Snapshot API: simplifies the audit-snapshot workflow.
Alternatives
  • Weaviate - similar features, heavier setup.
  • Milvus - excellent at billion scale, overkill for 3 M keyframes.
Use in our system

Backup vector store. Default to FAISS for latency; switch to Qdrant if the operator needs complex payload filters mid-contest.

BM25 (rank_bm25 / pyserini)

SPARSE RETRIEVAL

Classical lexical search over OCR, ASR, and captions.

What is it?

A probabilistic ranking function based on term frequency and inverse document frequency with tunable length normalisation. The canonical baseline for text retrieval.

Why we chose it
  • Catches exact terms CLIP misses ("Bộ Y tế", "Sài Gòn").
  • Trivially fast: indexes in seconds, queries in milliseconds.
  • Complementary to dense: BM25 + CLIP via RRF outperforms either alone.
Alternatives
  • SPLADE - learned sparse retrieval; great quality but slower.
  • Elasticsearch - heavier, network-bound, overkill.
Use in our system

Index OCR text, ASR transcripts, and BLIP-2 captions. Query in parallel with CLIP and fuse via RRF.

Reciprocal Rank Fusion (RRF)

FUSION

Combine the top-k of several rankers into one robust top-k.

What is it?

For each candidate i and each ranker j, the RRF score is Σ 1 / (k + rank_j(i)) with a small constant k (typically 60). Items ranked high by multiple rankers float to the top.

Why we chose it
  • No hyper-parameters beyond k; no learning required.
  • Robust: a single ranker disagreeing does not torpedo the consensus.
  • Standard in VBS-winning systems for fusing CLIP + BM25 + objects.
Alternatives
  • Learning-to-Rank (XGBoost / LambdaMART) - higher ceiling but needs training data.
  • CombSUM / Borda - simpler fusions; usually slightly worse than RRF.
Use in our system

Default fusion across CLIP, SigLIP (if enabled), BM25, and object filters. The fused top-100 feeds the rerank tier.

AI

3.4 LLM & Agent Layer

Plan ready; build is deferred until the BTC spec arrives (see ADR-003).

GPT-4o-mini (OpenAI API)

LLM · CLOUD

Cheap, fast, capable enough for query rewrite and rerank.

What is it?

OpenAI's small-but-capable model. Strong at instruction following, JSON output, and Vietnamese understanding. Low cost per token makes it acceptable for runtime use.

Why we chose it
  • JSON mode matches our strict prompt schemas (see artifacts/prompt-library/).
  • Sub-second latency for typical query rewrites.
  • Good Vietnamese: comparable to GPT-4o on basic comprehension.
Alternatives
  • Gemini 1.5 Flash - similar latency, alternative provider.
  • Claude 3 Haiku - strong on long context, slightly slower.
Use in our system

Tiers T1 (query rewrite) and T2 (rerank). Always behind a provider abstraction so we can swap.

Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct (local)

LLM · OFFLINE

Local fallback when the contest hall network falters.

What is it?

Alibaba's open-weight 7 B-parameter instruction-tuned LLM. Strong multilingual coverage including Vietnamese. Runs comfortably on a single 16 GB GPU at 4-bit quantisation.

Why we chose it
  • No network dependency: the contest hall has been unreliable in past years.
  • Open weights: no quota, no privacy concern about Vietnamese queries leaving the box.
  • Quality close enough to gpt-4o-mini for our prompt set.
Alternatives
  • Llama 3.1 8B - similar quality, weaker on Vietnamese.
  • Gemma 2 9B - good general model, restrictive licence.
Use in our system

Drop-in replacement for the API LLM under LOCAL_LLM_ENABLED=true. Same prompt schemas; same JSON output.

BE

3.5 Backend Stack

The async Python service that fronts the retrieval engine.

FastAPI + Uvicorn

WEB FRAMEWORK

Async-first Python web framework on the ASGI standard.

What is it?

FastAPI is a modern Python framework built on Starlette and Pydantic; it auto-generates OpenAPI docs from type hints. Uvicorn is the ASGI server that runs it.

Why we chose it
  • Type-safe by default: Pydantic validates every request and response.
  • Async: handle WebSocket + HTTP concurrently with one process.
  • OpenAPI: the frontend gets typed client code for free.
  • Lifespan: load the FAISS index once at startup, never per request.
Alternatives
  • Flask - older, synchronous; would block on long LLM calls.
  • Starlette directly - more verbose, loses auto-docs.
Use in our system

Hosts /api/v1/search, /submit, /video, plus WebSocket at /api/v1/ws/operator.

Pydantic v2 + pydantic-settings

DATA / CONFIG

Runtime-validated typed models and settings.

What is it?

Pydantic validates Python objects against type hints at runtime. v2 reimplements the core in Rust for major speed-ups. pydantic-settings reads typed config from .env + environment.

Why we chose it
  • Schema correctness: input from the frontend or BTC ingest is validated before it can crash the pipeline.
  • Speed: validates millions of records during ingest without bottlenecking.
  • Single source of truth: Pydantic models double as OpenAPI schemas and DTOs.
Use in our system

Every request/response body is a Pydantic model. Settings module is the only place os.getenv is allowed.

WebSocket (FastAPI)

REAL-TIME

Bidirectional, sub-50ms streaming for the operator UI.

What is it?

A persistent TCP-based channel for low-latency JSON messages. FastAPI's WebSocket support sits on the same event loop as HTTP.

Why we chose it
  • Result streaming: surface the first 12 candidates as they arrive instead of after the full top-100 is ranked.
  • Status updates: submission progress, rerank progress, audit-mode notifications.
  • Endorsed by the 2025 AI Challenge paper on real-time multimedia retrieval.
Use in our system

One persistent connection per operator; versioned JSON messages ({ "v": 1, "type": "result_chunk", "payload": ... }).

loguru + Prometheus + OpenTelemetry

OBSERVABILITY

Structured logs, metrics, and traces - the audit trail.

What are they?

loguru is a friendlier Python logger with structured kwargs. Prometheus scrapes a /metrics endpoint with our histograms and counters. OpenTelemetry instruments FastAPI for distributed traces.

Why we chose them
  • Audit-ready: every search and submission is captured with request_id.
  • Latency budgets: histograms expose where milliseconds go.
  • Post-round retros: replay any moment of a round from the logs.
Use in our system

Every log line carries request_id and endpoint. Prometheus runs locally; metrics dumped to reports/ after each round.

FE

3.6 Frontend Stack

A keyboard-first operator UI built on Once UI - the only design system allowed (see ADR-001).

Next.js 14 (App Router)

FRAMEWORK

React framework with server components and file-based routing.

What is it?

Next.js is a production-grade React framework. The App Router (since v13) supports Server Components, Streaming SSR, and co-located route segments.

Why we chose it
  • Server Components reduce JS sent to the browser - faster contest-day cold start.
  • App Router lets us colocate UI, loading states, and error boundaries.
  • Mature dev experience: fast refresh, typed images, file-based routes.
Alternatives
  • Vite + React Router - lighter but loses SSR and image optimisation.
  • Remix - excellent, but smaller ecosystem.
Use in our system

Hosts the operator UI at /, /search, /submit. Connects to FastAPI over HTTP + WebSocket.

Once UI

DESIGN SYSTEM

Semantic-layout, design-token-driven, dark-mode-first.

What is it?

A React component library that swaps utility classes for prop-driven layout primitives: <Column>, <Row>, <Grid>, <Heading>, <Text>. Tokens (background="surface", onBackground="neutral-weak") abstract colour and theme.

Why we chose it
  • Dark-mode-first: matches the contest-hall ergonomic setting.
  • Semantic layout: the JSX reads like a layout description, not a CSS class soup.
  • Vercel-/Linear-/Raycast-grade polish by default.
  • Accessibility on primitives without us reinventing it.
Banned alternatives
  • TailwindCSS / shadcn/ui - utility-class noise; rejected after ADR-001.
  • MUI / Chakra / Mantine - heavier, less coherent dark mode.
Use in our system

Every component is built from Once UI primitives. Custom product components live in frontend/components/ and spread Once UI props on the outer wrapper.

TanStack Query + Zustand

STATE

Server state and client state, separated.

What are they?

TanStack Query caches server data with stale-while-revalidate semantics. Zustand is a minimal client state library (no providers, no boilerplate).

Why we chose them
  • Right tool per kind of state: server data in TanStack Query, UI state in Zustand.
  • Cancellation: TanStack Query cancels in-flight queries on keystroke - critical for the search bar.
  • Devtools: easy debugging during mock contests.
Use in our system

One store per feature (useSearchStore, useShortcutsStore). Query hooks colocated in frontend/lib/queries/.

@tanstack/react-virtual

VIRTUALIZATION

Render thousands of thumbnails without freezing the browser.

What is it?

A headless virtualisation hook that renders only the visible portion of a long list or grid. Used for result grids of up to 10,000 keyframes.

Why we chose it
  • Headless: integrates cleanly with Once UI <Grid> without imposing styles.
  • Stable: shipped across the TanStack ecosystem.
  • Critical for our top-k=10000 investigation flows.
Use in our system

The result grid in components/search/ResultGrid.tsx wraps a virtualiser inside a Once UI <Grid>.

DB

3.7 Data & Processing

Formats, storage, and the pipes that move bytes.

Parquet + pandas + pyarrow

TABULAR

Columnar storage for OCR, ASR, objects, captions - billions of rows, fast.

What is it?

Parquet is a columnar binary file format with per-column compression and statistics. pandas + pyarrow let us read it with low memory and fast column-selective queries.

Why we chose it
  • 10x smaller than JSONL for our metadata.
  • Fast filtering: predicate push-down for video_id == "L01_V012".
  • Standard: integrates with FAISS, DuckDB, Spark if we scale up.
Use in our system

All data/metadata/*.parquet files. Built atomically (write tmp, rename). pandera or Pydantic checks at the boundary.

NumPy memmap + .npy

VECTORS

Memory-mapped float matrices for the embedding store.

What is it?

A NumPy memmap exposes a binary file on disk as a virtual array; pages load on access. The .npy header carries shape and dtype, making it self-describing.

Why we chose it
  • Instant startup: opening a 5 GB embedding file takes milliseconds.
  • Zero-copy: pass slices directly to FAISS.
  • Trivial backup: a single file per model variant.
Use in our system

One .npy per embedding model in data/index/<model>/embeddings.npy, paired with a JSON ids.json.

decord + PyAV + ffmpeg

VIDEO I/O

Fast random-access video reading without bash gymnastics.

What are they?

decord is a Python video reader with GPU-accelerated decoding. PyAV wraps FFmpeg for fine-grained control. ffmpeg is the swiss-army CLI underneath.

Why we chose them
  • Random access: jump to any timestamp without re-decoding the whole file.
  • Multi-thread: parallelise ingest across CPU cores.
  • Audio extraction: ffmpeg pipes audio into Whisper without intermediate WAVs.
Use in our system

Keyframe extraction, hover-preview clip generation, audio extraction for ASR.

DX

3.8 Development Tooling

The plumbing that keeps the workspace clean and fast.

uv + ruff + black

PYTHON DX

Fast deps, fast lints, predictable formatting.

What are they?

uv is a Rust-based Python package manager (5-10x faster than pip). ruff is a Rust-based linter that replaces flake8, isort, pyupgrade, and more. black is the deterministic formatter.

Why we chose them
  • Speed: every pip install becomes seconds, not minutes.
  • One linter: ruff covers what used to take 4 tools.
  • No style debate: black ends formatting bikeshedding.
Use in our system

Local dev: uv pip install -e .[dev]. CI / pre-commit: ruff check . --fix + black ..

pre-commit + gitleaks

QUALITY GATES

Catch bad commits before they leave the laptop.

What are they?

pre-commit orchestrates Git hooks declared in .pre-commit-config.yaml. gitleaks scans the diff for accidentally committed secrets (API keys, tokens).

Why we chose them
  • Defence in depth against leaked tokens, broken YAML, oversized files.
  • Tier-1 defence for risk R-18 (token leak via git).
  • Free quality: linting, large-file check, end-of-file fixer.
Use in our system

One-time setup: pre-commit install. Every commit auto-fixes whitespace, sorts imports, scans for secrets.

pytest + Vitest + Playwright

TESTING

Three frameworks, one philosophy: fail loud, fail fast.

What are they?

pytest for Python unit + integration tests, with httpx.AsyncClient for endpoint tests. Vitest + React Testing Library for frontend unit tests. Playwright for E2E browser flows.

Why we chose them
  • Coverage by layer: unit on internals, E2E on critical paths (search → select → submit).
  • Stable: each is the most-used option in its niche.
  • Audit value: a green test suite is evidence to the jury that the tool is the team's own.
Use in our system

pytest in tests/ with fixtures of 2-3 synthetic videos. Vitest beside frontend components. Playwright for keyboard-only navigation regression.

HuggingFace Hub + accelerate

MODELS

Pull models, run them efficiently across GPUs.

What are they?

HuggingFace Hub is the model registry; the huggingface_hub CLI pre-downloads weights into HF_HOME. accelerate wraps PyTorch for mixed-precision + multi-GPU inference with minimal code.

Why we chose them
  • Pre-warm: download weights once during ingest week; contest day is offline-tolerant.
  • Pinned revisions: prevents silent upstream changes mid-prep.
  • One mental model for every model (CLIP, BLIP-2, Qwen, ...).
Use in our system

All model weights cached under data/models/ (HF_HOME). from_pretrained("name", revision="<sha>") in every loader.
